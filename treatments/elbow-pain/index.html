Tennis and golfer’s elbow

Tennis elbow

Elbow pain is often due to tennis elbow, which affects one-third of people at some point in their lives.

Tennis elbow, which results in pain on the outer sides of the elbow, is medically known as lateral epicondylitis. It’s also referred to as tendonitis, because tendons swell and become painful after trauma.

Contrary to the condition’s popular name, you can get tennis elbow without ever picking up a racquet.

Tennis elbow can develop through any activity that puts repeated stress on the joint. It frequently happens after strenuous use of tendons and muscles in the forearm, near the elbow joint.

Thus, you can get tennis elbow from activities such as gardening or carpentry.

Symptoms indicating that tennis elbow treatment may be required include:

  • Pain in the forearm, just beneath the bend of the elbow.
  • Difficulty bending or lifting your arm.
  • Problems with gripping small objects like a pen.
  • Difficulty twisting your forearm, such as turning a door handle or opening a jar.
  • Pain when you fully extend your arm.

Golfer’s elbow

Golfer’s elbow is less common than tennis elbow.

Just as you don’t have to play tennis to get tennis elbow, you can develop golfer’s elbow without ever touching a golf club.

Golfer’s elbow is technically known as medial epicondylitis, a condition caused by constant tight gripping or repeated bending of the wrist. These activities result in tendons of the forearm muscles attaching to the elbow bone.

Symptoms of golfer’s elbow include:

  • Soreness on the inner side of the elbow, sometimes extending to the forearm.
  • Stiffness of the elbow.
  • Weakness in the wrists and hands.
  • Tingling or numbness that can spread to the fingers.

Natural golfer’s and tennis elbow pain treatments

Without treatment, pain from golfer’s elbow and tennis elbow can continue for several months or even longer. This is because tendons heal slowly, making it difficult to do basic everyday tasks such as turning a key in a door.

People with elbow pain who want to avoid potentially harmful steroids are increasingly turning to alternative forms of treatment.

Natural, drug-free solutions for elbow pain relief include:

  • Ice treatment
  • Rest
  • Exercise programmes
  • Massage
  • Myofascial release
  • Joint manipulation
